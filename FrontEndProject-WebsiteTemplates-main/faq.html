Frequently Asked Questions




Q: What if I already know what I want to go in?

A: Simply use the discovery chatbot to get faster counseling.


Q: How can I develop skills that are important for any career?

A: You can develop important career skills such as communication, problem-solving, teamwork, and time management through activities like school projects, extracurricular activities, part-time jobs, and volunteering.


Q: What are some scholarships and financial aid options available for pursuing higher education?

A: There are various scholarships, grants, and financial aid options available for high school students pursuing higher education. These include merit-based scholarships, need-based aid, grants from universities, and federal financial aid programs like FAFSA. Consider using the Discovery Bot in the Diagnostic section to get more options relevant to your career choice.


Q: How can I gain work experience while still in high school?

A: You can gain work experience in high school through part-time jobs, internships, job shadowing, volunteering, and participating in school-sponsored career exploration programs.


Q: What are some in-demand careers that will be relevant in the future?

A: Careers in fields like healthcare (e.g., nursing, medical technology), technology (e.g., software development, cybersecurity), renewable energy, environmental science, and data analytics are expected to be in demand in the future.


Q: How can I decide which career path is right for me?

A: You can decide on a career path by assessing your interests, strengths, and values. Research different careers, talk to professionals in those fields, and consider your long-term goals to make an informed decision. The counselor bot in the diagnostic section can provide helpful insight aswell.


Q: What steps can I take in high school to prepare for my future career?

A: To prepare for your future career, consider taking relevant courses, participating in extracurricular activities related to your interests, seeking internships or volunteer opportunities, and exploring your career interests through research and networking.
